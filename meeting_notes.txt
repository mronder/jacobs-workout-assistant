# Team Meeting: "Project Titan" - AI Workout Generator

**Date:** Oct 24, 2025
**Topic:** Architecture & UX for "The Ultimate Free Workout Planner"
**Attendees:** 12 (3 Exercise Scientists, 3 Personal Trainers, 2 Physical Therapists, 3 UI/UX Designers, 1 Lead Engineer)

---

## Transcript Summary

**Dr. Aris (PhD, Exercise Physiology):**
"Alright team, the client wants a robust workout generator. Key inputs are non-negotiable: Age, Height, Weight, Body Type, Frequency, Intensity, and Duration. From a physiological standpoint, we need to ensure the 'Body Type' selection actually influences the volume calculation. A 'slim' ectomorph needs different hypertrophy volume than a 'fat' endomorph looking for recomposition."

**Coach Mike (Head PT):**
"Agreed. And regarding the 'scrape the web' requirement—let's be real. We don't want to literally scrape random blogs. We want the *knowledge* from the best sources. We should use the Gemini model as our 'Synthesizer'. It's read every strength training study and forum. We'll prompt it to act as a meta-analyst."

**Sarah (Physical Therapist):**
"Please, for the love of joints, can we ensure the 'Intensity' toggle adjusts the *selection* of exercises, not just the reps? If a beginner selects 'High Intensity', don't give them plyometric box jumps immediately. Give them high-intensity low-impact intervals. We need a safety layer in the prompt."

**Leo (Lead UI/UX):**
"Visually, this needs to feel premium but accessible. I'm thinking 'Medical Grade Minimal'. Lots of whitespace, crisp typography (Inter + JetBrains Mono for data). The input phase should be a 'Wizard'—one question at a time, smooth transitions. Don't dump a giant form on them."

**Maya (Senior UX Researcher):**
"Right. And the payoff—the actual plan—needs to look like a tangible schedule. A calendar view or a timeline. Users need to visualize their commitment."

**Dev Lead Sam:**
"Technically, we'll use React with Tailwind. For the 'scraping' aspect, we'll leverage Gemini's grounding capabilities or just its internal knowledge base which is effectively a compressed version of the web's fitness knowledge. It's faster and less error-prone than building a custom scraper for Bodybuilding.com. We'll structure the output as JSON so we can render it beautifully, not just a wall of text."

**Dr. Aris:**
"One more thing: 'Body Type' is scientifically controversial (somatotypes are a bit dated), but users understand it. Let's map 'Fat' to 'Weight Loss/Metabolic Conditioning' focus, and 'Slim' to 'Hypertrophy/Strength' focus internally."

**Coach Mike:**
"And 'Intensity'? Let's map it to RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion). Low = RPE 5-6, High = RPE 8-9."

**Sarah (PT):**
"I want to see dynamic warmups included. No plan is complete without them."

**Leo (UI):**
"I'll design a 'Generation' screen that builds anticipation. 'Analyzing body type...', 'Scanning exercise databases...', 'Constructing periodization...'—make the user feel the AI is working hard."

**Manager:**
"Budget is $0 for user. We use the Gemini API key provided. No external paid DBs. Go."

---

## Consensus Architecture

1.  **Frontend:** React + Vite + Tailwind CSS.
2.  **State:** React Context for the multi-step wizard data.
3.  **AI Engine:** Gemini Flash (fast) or Pro (reasoning) to generate the JSON plan.
4.  **Prompt Engineering:** System instruction must act as a master trainer, incorporating safety, progression, and specificity.
5.  **Visuals:** Dark mode option, clean cards, progress bars for the wizard.
